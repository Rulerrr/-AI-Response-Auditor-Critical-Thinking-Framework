## PHASE 1: SYSTEMIC & ARCHITECTURAL PARADOXES
Analyse if the response falls into these systemic traps:
- **Braess Paradox:** Improvements in one part that degrade the entire system.
- **Goodhart's Law:** Optimising for a safe metric (pattern-matched answer) instead of the actual goal.
- **Jevons Paradox:** Efficiency gains that lead to higher total negative consumption.
- **Automation Paradox:** Reliance on patterns while losing human-level critical judgment.
- **Cobra Effect:** Solutions that create worse incentives or unintended negative results.
- **Transparency Paradox:** Optimising "appearance" of honesty instead of actual truth.
- **Abilene Paradox:** Providing a consensus-driven answer that nobody actually believes.
- **Simpson's Paradox:** Conclusions from data that would flip if aggregated differently.
- **Paradox of Tolerance:** Unlimited tolerance for illogical premises that destroys logic itself.
- **Innovation Illusion:** Claiming "modernity" or "AI power" as proof of quality.

## PHASE 2: LOGICAL & CAUSAL AUDIT
Scan for classic logic failures:
- **Post Hoc Ergo Propter Hoc:** Mistaking a sequence for a cause.
- **Circular Reasoning:** The argument relies on its own assumptions.
- **False Dilemma:** Limiting options when more exist.
- **Golden Mean Fallacy:** Forcing a compromise where one side is objectively wrong.
- **Argument to Ignorance:** Claiming truth just because something hasn't been proven false.
- **Slippery Slope:** Predicting a disaster chain without a solid causal link.
- **Texas Sharpshooter:** Picking data (like historical dye costs) to fit a specific conclusion.

- **Semantic Leakage:** CONTAMINATION ALERT. Did cultural/linguistic associations dictate logic without evidence?
- **Sycophancy Audit:** Simply agreeing with the user bias instead of being objective.
- **Leading Question Bias:** Letting the user's prompt dictate the logic path.
- **Straw Man:** Misrepresenting intent to make it easier to answer.
- **Ad Hominem Deflection:** Shifting focus to the user instead of the argument.
- **Ambiguity:** Using vague terms to avoid precise answers.
- **Appeal to Authority/Majority:** Justifying via status or consensus.
- **Whataboutism:** Deflecting criticism by pointing to other examples.
- **Wishful Thinking:** Accepting desires as facts.

## PHASE 4: COGNITIVE & STATISTICAL BIAS
- **Base Rate Fallacy:** Ignoring general statistics (e.g., millions like a color) for flashy anecdotes.
- **Survivor Bias:** Focusing only on success stories while ignoring failures.
- **Dunning-Kruger Trap:** Overconfidence in areas of high complexity/missing data.
